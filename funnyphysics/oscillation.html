The Distance of Sun.

Mostly:

  • 8 minutes and 20 seconds for the light to reach us.
  • Its temperature is estimated at 15 million Kelvin degrees (°K) and its density at 150 gm/cm3.
  • The hydrogen contained in the core of the Sun is ionized as protons, which fuse to form atomic nuclei of helium, releasing energy in the process.
  • The surface of the Sun is at 5,982 degrees Celsius.

Details:

  1. Sometimes, the flares heat the surface of the Sun, which reaches temperatures of about 45 million degrees Celsius, much higher than in the core.
  2. In its nucleus, constant nuclear reactions take place and they are the ones that produce the light and heat that we feel from our Planet.
  3. Conditions in the core of the new Sun are more extreme: there is higher pressure and higher temperature (due to gravitational compression)
  4. Although from Earth we perceive the Sun as yellow, the collection of gases, such as helium, hydrogen and other elements of which it is made, is so hot that it emits white light.

The distance of Planet.

Mostly:

  • Our home belonging to a family of eight planets called the Solar System, our planet being the third closest to the Sun.
  • Neptune is located at a distance of 4345.4 million kilometers. Being therefore the planet farthest from the earth.
  • The planet Farfarout is located 20 billion kilometers from our solar system, about 132 astronomical units.

Details:

  1. Our home, planet Earth, is a terrestrial, rocky planet.
  2. It has a solid and active surface, with mountains, valleys, canyons, plains and much more.
  3. Earth is special because it is an ocean planet, with water covering 70% of its surface.
  4. Our atmosphere is composed largely of nitrogen.
  5. The distance from the Sun, its size and density, the presence of bioelements on its surface and the Earth's magnetic field. make it habitable.

The distance of Stars.

Mostly:

  • With the help of the Hubble Space Telescope we will see enormous concentrations of stars, gases and dust; that are scattered in it like grains of sand.
  • Hubble's law indicates that galaxies are moving away from each other and that the universe is expanding.
  • In the standard model of the universe, a big bang gave rise to the first fundamental particles which later coalesced, forming stars and other astronomical bodies.

Details:

  1. The cosmic background radiation is a relic of the moment when atoms were formed
  2. Inside stars, the heaviest elements are formed due to nuclear fusion reactions.
  3. When looking at the sky, we see many stars about 2500 approximately from each hemisphere.
  4. The light we see from stars today was emitted by the star a long time ago, so it is a look into the past.
  5. A gravitational lens distorts and amplifies the image of distant galaxies producing double or multiple images, arcs, etc.
